# SSDI & Medicare Continuation Rules

**Key question:** If a client loses their disability cash benefits (SSDI), do they lose Medicare?

**Short answer:** Not always right away. It depends entirely on *why* SSDI was stopped.

---

## Why and When Medicare Stops

### 1. Stopped because of work (Substantial Gainful Activity)
If cash benefits stop because your client went back to work and earns too much, they can usually **keep their free Medicare Part A and Part B for at least 93 months (7 years and 9 months)** from the start of their Trial Work Period — as long as their disabling condition still meets safety rules.

### 2. Stopped because of medical improvement
If Social Security reviews the case and decides the client's medical condition has improved enough that they are no longer disabled, **Medicare generally ends shortly after the disability cash benefits stop.**

### 3. Age exception
If your client turns **65 while on disability**, their Medicare switches to regular age-based Medicare. This coverage **cannot be taken away** just because their disability status changes.

---

## Next Steps for Your Client

1. **Check the official notice letter** from the Social Security Administration to see the exact reason why disability benefits were stopped.
2. **Look for a specific end date** listed for their medical or hospital coverage.

---

## Key Questions to Ask the Client
- Why was their disability stopped — did they return to work, or did their medical condition improve?
- How old are they? (If 65+, they're protected under age-based Medicare)

These two answers determine exactly how long they can keep Medicare coverage.
