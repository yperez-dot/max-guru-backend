# Social Security Benefits Calculator (AARP)

**Tool:** AARP Social Security Calculator
**Link:** https://www.aarp.org/social-security/benefits-calculator/

---

## What the AARP Calculator Does

Estimates your **monthly Social Security retirement benefit** based on:
- Your earnings history
- The age you plan to claim

It also shows:
- What percentage of daily expenses your benefit can cover
- How waiting to collect **increases** your monthly payment
- How continued work after claiming affects your benefit

---

## How Social Security Calculates Your Retirement Benefit

SSA uses your **35 highest-earning years**, adjusted for historical wage growth. Years with no earnings count as zeros, which lowers the average.

Agents tip: Clients can review their full earnings history by creating a **My Social Security account** at ssa.gov.

---

## What the Calculator Does NOT Cover

The AARP calculator only estimates **retirement** benefits. It does **not** calculate:

- **SSDI (Social Security Disability Insurance)** — for people who can't work due to a severe medical condition expected to last at least 1 year or result in death. Benefits continue only as long as the disability prevents earning a living.
- **SSI (Supplemental Security Income)** — a separate program for people 65+ or with disabilities who have little or no income/assets.

### 2026 SSI Maximum Monthly Payments
| Situation | Monthly Maximum |
|---|---|
| Single person | $994 |
| Married couple (both qualify) | $1,491 |

Note: Some states supplement the federal SSI amount. Actual payment may be less if the client or family has other income.

---

## The Three-Legged Stool (Retirement Context)

Social Security was never designed to be a retiree's only income. The traditional U.S. retirement system is:
1. **Social Security**
2. **Pension** (increasingly replaced by 401k/tax-deferred plans)
3. **Personal savings**

Employers have largely shifted away from pensions toward 401(k)s, making individual savings increasingly important.

---

## Agent Use Case

When clients ask "How much will I get from Social Security?" — direct them to the AARP calculator for a retirement estimate, and clarify that SSDI and SSI are separate programs with different eligibility rules.
