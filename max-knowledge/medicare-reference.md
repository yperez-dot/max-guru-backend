# 1. 2026 Medicare Costs & Premiums

> Source: CMS.gov 2026 Medicare Parts A & B Premiums and Deductibles fact sheet, verified via web search | As of: 2026 plan year, figures finalized November 2025

## Part B

- **Standard Monthlypremium:** $202.90 (up from $185.00 in 2025)
- **Annual Deductible:** $283 (up from $257 in 2025)
- **Irmaa Range:** $284.10 to $689.90/month depending on income

## Part A

- **Premiumfull Rate:** $565/month (fewer than 30 quarters of coverage)
- **Premiumreduced Rate:** $311/month (30+ quarters of coverage)
- **Inpatient Hospital Coinsurance:** $434/day for days 61-90, $868/day for lifetime reserve days
- **Skilled Nursing Coinsurance:** $217/day for days 21-100

## Part D

- **Average Base Premium:** $38.99 (up from $36.78 in 2025) -- varies by plan
- **Irmaarange:** $14.50 to $91.00/month additional, on top of plan's own premium

## IRMAA Thresholds (2026)

- **Individual:** Standard rate applies up to $109,000 MAGI; surcharges begin above that
- **Joint Filing:** Standard rate applies up to $218,000 MAGI; surcharges begin above that
- **Based On:** MAGI from 2 years prior (2026 IRMAA uses 2024 tax returns)
- **Note:** Multiple tiers exist between the threshold and the top surcharge -- don't state a single flat surcharge amount without checking the tier.

## Immunosuppressive Drug Coverage (Post-Transplant)

- **Standard Premium:** $121.60/month
- **Highest Tier Premium:** $608.10/month for highest earners

# 2. PY2027 Regulatory Changes

> Note: Plan Year 2027 regulatory changes, summarized in THEI's own words from AHIP certification training content (not reproduced verbatim -- AHIP's training materials are copyrighted). Facts only.

## TPMO Disclaimer — Timing Change

- **Timingchange:** Disclaimer no longer required within the first minute of a call -- now just needs to be provided before any discussion of benefits
- **Contentchange:** No longer references SHIPs as an additional information source
- **Electronicrequirement:** Still required verbally on calls, electronically for email/chat, and prominently on TPMO websites and marketing materials

## Scope of Appointment (SOA) — Changes

- **Waiteliminated:** The 48-hour wait after SOA completion is eliminated -- SOA just needs to be completed before the personal marketing appointment, not 48 hours in advance
- **Eventflexibility:** SOA forms may now be distributed and collected at educational events (previously restricted)

## Marketing & Educational Events — Same-Space Rule

- **Samespacerule:** Marketing events may now be held in the same space immediately after an educational event -- beneficiaries just need to be informed the educational event ended and given time to leave before the marketing event starts

## 2027 Maximum Out-of-Pocket (MOOP) Figures

- **Coordinatedcareandpffs:** $9,850 maximum (most plans set lower)
- **Ppoaggregatenetworkandoon:** $14,800 maximum (most plans set lower)

## Part D Standard Benefit (2027)

- **Deductible:** $700
- **Initialcoveragephasecoinsurance:** 25% of drug costs
- **Catastrophicthreshold:** $2,400 out-of-pocket (TrOOP) before reaching catastrophic phase, where enrollee pays $0
- **Insulincap:** $35/month max in any phase, not subject to deductible

## LIS / Extra Help Income Thresholds (2027)

- **Fbdeatorbelow100Pctfpl:** $0 deductible, $1.65 generic / $5.00 other drugs cost-sharing up to OOP threshold ($0 if institutionalized/HCBS)
- **Fbdeabove100Pctfpl:** $0 deductible, $5.80 generic / $14.40 other drugs
- **Lisnonfbde:** $0 deductible, $5.80 generic / $14.40 other drugs

## Superlative Claims Rule Change

CMS no longer requires sourcing/documentation citations for superlative claims (e.g. 'highest rated') in materials -- but using false, inaccurate, or misleading superlatives is still prohibited

## Prior Authorization Transparency (2027)

MA organizations must post on their websites: services requiring prior auth, approval/denial percentages, and average/median decision time

# 3. SNP Types

## C-SNP (Chronic Condition Special Needs Plan)

Chronic condition SNP -- restricted to beneficiaries with one or more CMS-specified severe/disabling chronic conditions (diabetes, ESRD, cardiovascular disorders, cancer, chronic lung disorders, HIV, dementia, autoimmune disorders, CKD, chronic heart failure, certain mental health disorders, drug/alcohol dependence, epilepsy/MS/Parkinson's, and others). Most common C-SNPs target cardiovascular, chronic heart failure, and diabetes.

## D-SNP (Dual-Eligible Special Needs Plan)

Dual-eligible SNP -- for beneficiaries eligible for both Medicare and Medicaid. Categories: QMB (Medicaid helps with Part A/B premiums, deductibles, coinsurance, copays), SLMB (Medicaid helps with Part B premium only), QI (Medicaid helps with Part B premium only), QDWI (Medicaid pays Part A premium for certain under-65 disabled individuals who return to work), FBDE/QMB-Plus (full Medicaid benefits). Eligibility categories vary by state.

### D-SNP Subtypes

FIDE SNPs (fully integrated Medicare+Medicaid under one org), HIDE SNPs (highly integrated, cover certain Medicaid benefits under capitated contract), Coordination-only D-SNPs (notify state Medicaid of hospital/SNF admissions, less integrated).

## I-SNP (Institutional Special Needs Plan)

Institutional SNP -- for individuals needing 90+ days of skilled nursing/nursing facility/long-term care level of care.

## IE-SNP (Institutional Equivalent SNP)

Institutional Equivalent SNP -- for community-dwelling individuals who need institutional level of care (e.g. certain assisted living facility residents).

# 4. Election Periods

## AEP — Annual Enrollment Period (Oct 15 – Dec 7)

Oct 15 - Dec 7 annually. Enroll, disenroll, or change MA/Part D plans. Last election made before AEP ends is what takes effect. Marketing reps cannot solicit/accept enrollment before Oct 15.

## MA-OEP — Medicare Advantage Open Enrollment Period (Jan 1 – Mar 31)

Jan 1 - Mar 31 for those enrolled in MA on Jan 1. One plan change allowed. Can switch MA plans, drop MA for Original Medicare, or add/drop Part D coverage. NOT available for MSA/Cost Plan/PACE enrollees. Marketing reps cannot target/reference the OEP.

## ICEP — Initial Coverage Election Period

Initial Coverage Election Period for MA -- begins 3 months before first entitled to Part A + enrolled in Part B, ends later of (2 months after that entitlement) or (end of Part B initial enrollment period).

## Part D IEP — Initial Enrollment Period

Part D Initial Enrollment Period -- begins 3 months before first entitled to Part A OR enrolled in Part B, ends 3 months after.

## SEP — Change of Residence

Move out of service area or gain new options from permanent move -- SEP begins at move (or month before if notified in advance), ends 2 months after the move month.

## SEP — 5-Star Plan

Available Dec 8 - Nov 30 each year for beneficiaries in a 5-star plan's service area; usable once.

## SEP — Dual Eligible / LIS

Dual eligibles and LIS-qualified (non-Medicaid) individuals can elect a standalone PDP once per month, effective the 1st of the following month. Does NOT allow MA-PD enrollment/changes. Unavailable to 'at-risk' beneficiaries for drug misuse.

## SEP — Chronic Condition (C-SNP)

Beneficiaries with a qualifying severe/disabling chronic condition can enroll in a matching C-SNP; lasts as long as they have the condition.

## SEP — Network Change

For MA enrollees whose provider/facility is being terminated from the network -- begins the month notified, extends 2 more months.

## OEP-I — Open Enrollment for Institutionalized Individuals

Open Enrollment Period for Institutionalized individuals -- continuous while residing in a qualifying institution (not assisted living), ends 2 months after leaving. Unlimited MA enrollment requests allowed.

# 5. Part D Formulary Requirements

### Formularyrequirements

Must include at least 2 drugs per therapeutic category (with exceptions), generic + brand options, adequate coverage per national treatment guidelines, all/substantially-all 'protected class' drugs.

### Protecteddrugclasses

Antidepressants, antipsychotics, anticonvulsants, immunosuppressants (transplant rejection), antiretrovirals, antineoplastics -- these must be on every formulary.

### Tieringtypical

Tier 1: generics (lowest cost-share). Tier 2: preferred brand. Tier 3: non-preferred brand. Tier 4: high-cost/specialty (plans may have 2 specialty tiers).

### Standardbenefit2027

See MEDICARE_2026_COSTS / PY2027_UPDATES for exact dollar figures ($700 deductible, 25% initial coinsurance, $2,400 TrOOP threshold to catastrophic).

### Steptherapyandpriorauth

Step therapy requires trying a lower-cost drug first; prior auth requires doctor to justify medical necessity to the plan before coverage.

### Transitionrefill

New enrollees/plan-switchers get at least a one-month supply of a non-formulary drug they were already taking, during the first 90 days, without prior auth/step therapy/quantity limits applying yet.

### Lateenrollmentpenalty

1% of national average beneficiary premium per month without Part D or creditable coverage after IEP, generally applies for as long as they have Part D. LIS recipients are exempt.

# 6. TPMO Marketing Rules

### Unsolicitedcontactban

No door-to-door, no approaching in common areas (lobbies/parking lots/etc.), no cold calls without a prior SOA/business reply card, no unsolicited texts/social messages, no voicemails after unsolicited calls.

### Giftlimits

$15 max per individual item (retail value), $75 max aggregate per beneficiary per year. No cash or cash-equivalents (gift cards usable anywhere/for anything, like Visa gift cards, count as cash-equivalent; a Starbucks or gas-station-specific card does not).

### Educationalvsmarketingevents

Educational events cannot include plan-specific premiums/benefits/ratings or enrollment forms. Marketing events can include all of that. As of PY2027, a marketing event can immediately follow an educational event in the same space, as long as attendees are told the educational portion ended and given time to leave first.

### Starratingrules

Can only cite an individual category rating alongside the plan's overall rating (can't cherry-pick a good category score to imply the whole plan is that good). Must stop using an old Star Rating within 21 days of a new one being released.

### Prohibitedclaims

Can't call MA the same as Medigap/Original Medicare, can't imply CMS/Medicare endorses the plan, can't use 'free' for a $0 premium, can't use misleading superlatives (best/highest-rated/etc. without support -- though CMS no longer requires citing sources for superlatives as of PY2027, false ones are still banned).

### Referralfeecap

$100 max for a referral resulting in MA/MA-PD/Cost Plan enrollment, $25 max for PDP.

# 7. Appeals & Grievances

### Grievance

Complaint about plan/provider operations (timeliness, access, quality) -- oral, written, or via medicare.gov complaint form.

### Coveragedecision

Plan's determination of whether something is covered and at what cost -- needed for prior auth, formulary exceptions, continuing a service, etc.

### Appeal

Formal request to reverse a coverage/payment decision. Enrollee or (with authorization) their physician can file.

### Priorauthtimeline

Standard MA prior auth decision required within 7 days (72 hours for drugs), faster if delay would endanger health.

### Formularyexception

Enrollee can request a non-formulary drug be covered, or a formulary drug be covered at a lower tier (tiering exception) -- requires physician supporting statement.

# 8. Plan Type Mechanics

## HMO (Health Maintenance Organization)

Network-based, may require PCP + referrals for specialists. Some have a POS option allowing limited out-of-network access.

## PPO (Preferred Provider Organization)

Any provider accepting Medicare + the plan, no referral needed, higher cost-sharing out-of-network. Regional PPOs span multiple states.

## PFFS (Private Fee-For-Service)

Any Medicare-accepting provider who agrees to the plan's terms per-visit. No prior auth/referrals required. May balance-bill up to 15% if plan allows it.

## MSA (Medical Savings Account)

High-deductible plan + Medicare-funded savings account. No premium. Does NOT cover Part D (must get a standalone PDP). 2026 max deductible was $18,100 (most plans lower). Ineligible if you have other coverage covering the deductible, VA/FEHB benefits, or have elected hospice.
