### Commission Rules by Enrollment Type

**D-SNP & C-SNP:**
- Renewals → ✅ 2026 FMV commission
- New enrollments → ✅ 2026 FMV commission (except the 4 Simply Level plans above = ❌ non-commissionable)

**HMO non-SNP:**
- Renewals → ✅ 2026 FMV renewal commission
- New enrollments → ✅ **2025 FMV commission** (note: 2025 rate applies, not 2026)
- Exception: Simply More H5471-077 → ❌ non-commissionable for new sales

### How to enroll on non-commissionable plans (if client insists)
Non-commissionable and non-lead plans are **not available on broker quoting/enrollment platforms** (Sunfire, Connecture, etc.).  
They can only be viewed at the plan's website or at [Medicare.gov](https://www.medicare.gov).

## Quick Agent Reference

**Q: Can I write a new Simply Level or Simply More?**  
A: No. Simply Level (H5471-069, H5471-073, H5471-075, H5471-080) and Simply More (H5471-077) are non-commissionable for new sales as of 1/1/2026. Enroll new members in commissionable alternatives instead.

**Q: What commission rate do I get on new non-SNP HMO enrollments?**  
A: 2025 FMV commission rate (not 2026). The 2026 FMV rate only applies to D-SNP/C-SNP new enrollments and all renewals.

**Q: My existing member is on Simply Extra H5471-104 in Broward. What happens?**  
A: They auto-transition to Simply Extra Platinum H5471-123 on 1/1/2026. No action needed.

**Q: Will I get paid on Simply Level renewals?**  
A: Yes — 2026 FMV commission applies to renewals of non-commissionable plans. Only NEW sales are non-commissionable.

**Q: Where can I view non-commissionable plans if a client asks about them?**  
A: Medicare.gov or the Elevance/Simply Healthcare plan website. They won't appear on quoting platforms like Sunfire.
